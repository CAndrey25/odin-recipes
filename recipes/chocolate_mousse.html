Chocolate Mousse

Description

This is the recipe for a delicious chocolate mousse that is very easy to make even for beginners.

But even the most experienced in the kitchen should try this recipe because it is one of the most perfectly textured chocolate mousses I've tried.

Ingredients

  • 3 1/2 ounces dark chocolate (62% cacao is ideal)
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • 2 large egg yolks
  • 1 tablespoon white sugar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 tiny pinch salt
  • 1/2 cup chilled heavy whipping cream

Steps

  1. First Step

    2. Break up or chop chocolate into small pieces and set aside with butter.

  2. The second step

    3. Add egg yolks, sugar, water, and salt to a metal mixing bowl. Cook, whisking, directly over medium-low heat until the mixture is thick, foamy, and hot to the touch (145 to 150 degrees F (63 to 65 degrees C)).

  3. The third step

    4. Once the yolk mixture is thick and hot, add chocolate and butter, and whisk until all chocolate is melted. Let rest for a few minutes on the counter, whisking occasionally to further cool the mixture to just above or at room temperature. The chocolate mixture shouldnâ€™t go into the whipped cream while still warm, but if cooled too long, the mixture may get too firm to fold in.

  4. The fourth step

    5. Whisk cold cream until medium stiff peaks form. If cream is whisked further, it will separate and the final texture will be grainy.

  5. The fifth step

    6. Transfer about 1/3 of chocolate mixture into whipped cream, and fold with a spatula until almost incorporated. Gently fold in remaining chocolate, trying to keep as much air in the mixture as possible.

  6. The sixth step

    7. Transfer into 4 serving dishes, wrap, and chill before serving, at least 1 hour.
